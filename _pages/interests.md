One of my research interests is the entanglement renormalization and tensor network states. The merge of the entanglement and renormalization group was proposed by Steven White in his famous paper “Density matrix formulation for quantum renormalization groups” in the 1990s.His algorithm Density Matrix Renormalization Group(DMRG) successfully solved the impurity Kondo problem which the usual renormalization approach could not. He provided a new insight on how entanglement structure tells us what to discard during each RG process. Under the development within these decades, DMRG can now be recast in tensor network operators and a generical pure quantum many body states can also be regarded as tensor network states.

![image info](/assets/images/ZER.jpg)

Now we have different algorithms aimed at representing the states with different entanglement s tructures(area Law, Log(L)) such as Projected Entangled Pair States(PEPS) and Multiscale Entanglement Renormalization Ansatz(MERA). They can efficiently represent a generic quantum state. Especially, MERA also opens a new window on high energy physics due to its conformal geometry(i.e. AdS/CFT correspondence).
